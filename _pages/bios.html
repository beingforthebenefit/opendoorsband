Gerald Todd

Keyboard/Organ

Gerald is a lifelong pianist who started playing at a young age. His love for classic rock has led him to play in many bands covering iconic bands such as the Beatles, the Kinks, and of course, The Doors. Gerald has played gigs all around southern California and Alaska, but now he's made his home in the Bay Area, where he's been captivating audiences with his keyboard skills.

Pato Milo

Guitar

Pato grew up playing bass and guitar in a variety of San Francisco Bay Area original and cover bands and loves to "playing to the room." Former bands include: Iguana Twist (MTV appearance on The Basement Tapes 1984), Big Mix, funk and soul; Moes Kitchen, acid jazz, funk, soul, Tumblin' Dice, A Rolling Stones Tribute, The Re-Tenders, Pretenders Tribute, Dead Cat Hat (indie rock, produced 4 albums), The Stolen Moments, a trio playing re-imagined covers. Pato also played upright and electric bass in a variety of jazz combos and theater pit orchestras, and with the 561st Air National Guard Band.

Ed Glantz

Drummer, backup vocals

Ed began his music odyssey with 10 years of piano lessons starting at age six. At age 10, he added drum lessons and never looked back. For the past 15 years, Ed has played in several Beatles and Rolling Stones tribute bands, as well as various other bands focusing on music from the 1950s through 1970s, including the notable Bay Area standouts Ticket To Ride and The Back Pages. In addition to drumming, Ed enjoys adding vocals harmonies to the mix. Now he's the drummer and bringing his magical chops and tempo into the Open Doors band.

John Arntz

Bassist, backup vocals

John Arntz has been a life-long musician and multi-instrumentalist (he spent some years teaching didjeridu....) and only started playing in bands in his 50's. Over the past decade he has started and managed a number of Sonoma County bands, covering rock, blues, country, and funk as well as hosting a monthly karaoke experience with a live band that takes on 20 vocalists for the evening. John is still working on his 10,000 hours as a musician and is delighted to be playing the role of Ray Manzarek's left hand in the Open Doors.
